Morgan Morning Drink

Serves: 1

Ingredients

  • Fruit (your choice)
  • 1 scoop protein powder (Nature’s Plus Soy)
  • 1 heaping tablespoon lecithin granules
  • 1 heaping tablespoon raw wheat germ
  • 1 heaping tablespoon flax seed
  • 3 capfuls aloe vera juice
  • 3 slices fresh ginger, cut in half
  • Sweetener to taste
  • Water to cover ingredients
  • 1/2 teaspoon green spirulina
  • 2 tablespoons psyllium husk colon cleanser

Directions

  1. Add fruit, protein powder, lecithin granules, wheat germ, flax seed, aloe vera juice, ginger, and sweetener to blender.
  2. Fill blender with water to cover all ingredients. Blend well.
  3. While blender is in motion, carefully add 1/2 teaspoon green spirulina.
  4. Last, add 2 tablespoons psyllium husk. Drink immediately as it will thicken quickly.

Bagna Caulda

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole garlic cloves
  • 1 can anchovy fillets
  • Olive oil
  • 1/4 pound butter
  • Heavy cream

Directions

  1. Boil garlic for 5–10 minutes. Drain and chop finely.
  2. Place finely chopped garlic in a saucepan and just cover with olive oil. Add the stick of butter and simmer this mixture for 10 minutes.
  3. Add cream to taste. Keep warm in a chafing dish and dip raw veggies.

Fresh Green Bean Casserole

Cook: 18–20 min

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds trimmed green beans
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 cups bread crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Salt & pepper

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 400°F.
  2. Fill a large bowl with ice water and set aside. Cook green beans in a large pot of boiling water until tender. Transfer beans to the ice water to cool, then drain.
  3. Heat olive oil in a pan and add garlic — cook until soft. Remove from heat.
  4. Toss bread crumbs, melted butter, salt, pepper, and blanched green beans together with the garlic.
  5. Bake until beans are tender, about 18–20 minutes.

Flank Steak

Serves: 4–6

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 pounds flank steak
  • 3–4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Combine balsamic vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, olive oil, and rosemary. Marinate steak (oil & vinegar marinade or all day).
  2. Grill or cook 5 minutes per side.
  3. Rest 10 minutes before slicing.

Chicken Cobbler

Ingredients

  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 red onion
  • 3 carrots
  • 1 large parsnip
  • 3 1/2 ounces shiitake mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons brandy
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • Milk
  • 3/4 cup pearl onions
  • 3/4 cup frozen peas
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons flat-leaf parsley
  • Pinch of fresh grated nutmeg

Directions

  1. Melt butter and sauté red onion, carrots, parsnip, and shiitake mushrooms until softened.
  2. Add brandy and cook off. Stir in flour and gradually add milk to form a sauce.
  3. Fold in pearl onions, peas, thyme, parsley, and nutmeg. Add cooked chicken.
  4. Top with cobbler dough or biscuit topping and bake until golden and bubbling.

Pepper Seared Duck Breast with Port Cherry Glaze

Serves: 4

Duck

  • 4 whole duck breasts
  • 2 tablespoons cracked black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt or kosher salt
  • 1 cup premium olive oil

Port Cherry Glaze

  • 1 bottle good-quality Port (not generic cooking port)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 24–30 dried Bing cherries or fresh pitted cherries

Directions

  1. For the duck: Divide each duck breast with a sharp fillet knife and carefully remove skin, making sure not to tear the breast meat. (It’s OK to leave skin on if you prefer.)
  2. Place duck in a bowl and coat with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Cover with plastic wrap and marinate for at least 4 hours.
  3. Preheat oven to 425°F. Heat a large cast iron skillet and sear duck breasts 2 minutes each side.
  4. Place skillet in oven and continue cooking 8–12 minutes depending on preference. Remove and let stand while finishing the glaze.
  5. For the glaze: In a medium saucepan, bring Port, bay leaves, and brown sugar to a boil. Reduce glaze mixture by half, until it has a thick syrup consistency.
  6. If using fresh cherries, pit them and add at the end. If using dried Bing cherries, add them right away. Sauce can be refrigerated for over a week.
  7. To serve: Make diagonal cuts in the cooked duck breasts and place over a favorite side dish, such as garlic mashed potatoes or asparagus. Pour approximately 3 tablespoons of glaze over each breast, equally dispersing cherries.

Chocolate Custard

Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs
  • 3/4 cup sugar (for custard)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla
  • 1/4 cup amaretto
  • Powder chocolate or cocoa
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups sugar (for caramel)

Directions

  1. Beat eggs, 3/4 cup sugar, vanilla, and amaretto together well.
  2. Stir in powder chocolate (or cocoa) and whole milk.
  3. Burn 2 cups sugar until caramelized and pour into custard pan.
  4. Add egg mixture to the pan. Place pan inside a water bath and bake until set.

Basic Yeast Cake

Cook: 20–30 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk
  • 2/3 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 6 tablespoons butter or margarine
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 packages Fleischmann’s Yeast (or 2 cakes)
  • 2 eggs, well beaten
  • 3 1/2 to 4 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • Grated lemon peel
  • Apple slices or crumbs (butter, sugar & flour) for topping

Directions

  1. Heat milk to a boil. Stir in sugar, salt, and butter. Cool to lukewarm.
  2. Measure 1/2 cup warm water into a cup. Crumble in yeast and stir until dissolved. Add to the lukewarm milk mixture.
  3. Stir in well-beaten eggs and slowly add 3 1/2 to 4 cups flour. Beat dough well.
  4. Add raisins and grated lemon peel. Let rise in a warm place until doubled in bulk.
  5. Beat dough again. Put into round or square buttered and floured baking forms. Dough should be about finger-thick. Let rise again.
  6. Top with apple slices or crumbs made from butter, sugar, and flour.
  7. Bake at 400°F for 20–30 minutes.

Marie’s Cheese Cake

Cook: 1 hr 20 min
Serves: 12

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shortbread cookie crumbs (with pecans)
  • 1 tablespoon plus 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 5 packages cream cheese
  • 5 eggs
  • 3 cups blueberries
  • Confectioners’ sugar for dusting

Directions

  1. Position rack in center of oven and preheat to 325°F. Lightly butter a cake pan. Cover outside with aluminum foil, shiny side out.
  2. In a bowl, stir together cookie crumbs and 1 tablespoon granulated sugar. Press crumb mixture into bottom and halfway up sides of prepared pan. Refrigerate until ready to use.
  3. In a bowl of an electric mixer with flat beater, beat cream cheese on high speed until smooth and creamy, 3–5 minutes.
  4. Reduce speed to low, gradually add 2 cups granulated sugar and beat 2–3 minutes, occasionally scraping down sides of bowl.
  5. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition and scraping down sides. Beat until smooth and creamy, 2–3 minutes.
  6. Pour filling into prepared pan. Scatter 1 cup blueberries over top.
  7. Bake until filling is just set, about 1 hour 20 minutes. Cool on a wire rack to room temperature, at least 1 hour. Refrigerate at least 4 hours before serving.
  8. Unmold cheesecake onto a dish. Garnish with remaining 2 cups blueberries and dust with confectioners’ sugar.

Cabbage Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 small leeks, chopped (white part only)
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2 tablespoons chopped garlic
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 large carrots, sliced
  • 3/4 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 2 small potatoes
  • Salt, pepper & fresh parsley
  • 4 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1 teaspoon Roquefort cheese per bowl, for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a soup pot. Add leeks, onions, and garlic and cook until softened.
  2. Stir in chicken stock, water, sliced carrots, and caraway seeds. Bring to a boil.
  3. Stir in potatoes. Reduce heat and simmer 15 minutes.
  4. Add salt, pepper, and fresh parsley. Stir in shredded cabbage.
  5. Continue to cook until cabbage and potatoes are done.
  6. Ladle into bowls and sprinkle each with 1 teaspoon Roquefort cheese. Serve.

Caesar Salad

Ingredients

  • 3 medium heads Romaine lettuce, washed, dried & chilled
  • 1/3 cup olive oil, room temperature
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 2–3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 raw egg
  • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce
  • Fresh pepper & salt
  • 1/2 can flat anchovy fillets
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup ready-made Caesar salad croutons

Directions

  1. Remove big ribs from lettuce and tear leaves into bite-size pieces.
  2. Combine olive oil, garlic, rice wine vinegar, lemon juice, raw egg, Worcestershire sauce, pepper, and salt in a blender. Run for 1 minute.
  3. Chop anchovy fillets and add to the blender dressing.
  4. Put salad into a bowl and pour dressing over it. Add Parmesan cheese and croutons. Toss everything together and serve.

Fresno Spinach Salad

Salad

  • 1 1/2 bunches spinach, well washed, stems removed
  • 1 medium white onion, sliced into rings
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1 jar red pimiento strips (mild)
  • 3 slices crisp fried bacon, crumbled

Dressing

  • 1/2 cup salad oil (Wesson)
  • 3 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon dry vermouth wine (important!)
  • 1/2 teaspoon powdered mustard or 1 teaspoon prepared mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sweet paprika powder

Directions

  1. Mix all dressing ingredients together.
  2. Pour dressing over spinach greens. Top with onion rings, sliced eggs, crumbled bacon, and pimiento strips.
  3. Toss and serve.

Perfection Salad

Ingredients

  • 2 envelopes unflavored gelatin
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups boiling water
  • 1 1/2 cups cold water
  • 1 cup rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cups finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup green pepper
  • 1/4 cup diced red pimiento
  • 1/3 cup green sliced olives
  • Carrot slices & black olives, for garnish
  • Mayonnaise, for serving

Directions

  1. Stir together gelatin, sugar, and boiling water until dissolved.
  2. Add cold water, rice wine vinegar, and lemon juice. Chill in a bowl until partly set.
  3. Fold in finely chopped cabbage, celery, green pepper, diced red pimiento, and sliced green olives.
  4. Stir all together with the gelatin mixture, pour into a mold or bowl, and chill in the refrigerator until firm.
  5. Garnish with carrot slices and black olives. Pass a bowl of mayonnaise on the side.

Mom’s Three Bean Soup

Total: about 3 1/2 hrs

Ingredients

  • 3 handfuls pinto beans
  • 1 handful lentils
  • 2 handfuls split peas
  • 3 beef neck bones
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 onions
  • 3 carrots
  • 4 stalks celery
  • 5 potatoes
  • 1 can tomato sauce

BLT Spaghetti

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 1 cup pancetta, diced
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, shredded
  • 1/2 cup garlic, chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Arugula
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 pints cherry tomatoes

Directions

  1. Cook spaghetti according to package directions. Drain.
  2. Crisp pancetta in olive oil. Add diced red onion and garlic, cook until softened.
  3. Halve cherry tomatoes and add to the pan. Toss with hot pasta.
  4. Fold in arugula, fresh basil, and Parmigiano-Reggiano. Serve immediately.

Chili Verde

Cook: 2+ hrs

Ingredients

  • Pork or beef, cubed
  • Flour for dredging
  • Chopped onion
  • Chopped tomatillos
  • Hot peppers
  • Ortega chiles, chopped
  • Salt & pepper

Directions

  1. Dredge pork (or beef) in flour and season well.
  2. Brown the onion and meat together.
  3. Add tomatillos, hot peppers, and Ortega chiles. Simmer, adding water until desired consistency is reached.
  4. Cook approximately 2 hours or more. The longer the better.

Shortcut: Use Herdez Mexican green hot sauce instead of tomatillos, peppers, and Ortegas.
