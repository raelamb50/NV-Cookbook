Goat Cheese and Herb Stuffed Radicchio Leaves

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 5 min
Level: Intermediate
Serves: 12 stuffed leaves

Ingredients

  • 1 cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 3 scallions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 11 ounces goat cheese, frozen for 15 minutes and sliced into 12 (1/2-inch) pieces
  • 12 radicchio leaves, cleaned

Directions

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat until a deep-fry thermometer registers 300°F. Remove from heat and allow to cool for 5 minutes.
  2. Combine the garlic, parsley, basil, thyme, and scallions in a small, heat-resistant glass bowl. Carefully pour the warm oil over the herb mixture. Add the salt and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Spoon a thin layer of the herb mixture on the bottom of an 8 by 8-inch square glass baking dish. Arrange the goat cheese slices in a single layer on top. Spoon the remaining herb mixture evenly over the goat cheese.
  4. Cover the baking dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  5. To serve: Place the radicchio leaves on a serving platter; carefully place a slice of marinated goat cheese in each leaf. Drizzle with additional herb mixture and serve.

Italian Lentil Salad

Prep: 8 min
Cook: 20 min
Level: Easy
Serves: 4 to 6

Salad

  • 1 pound green lentils (recommended: Sabarot)
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 1 cup halved seedless green grapes
  • 1 cup halved seedless red grapes
  • 1 cucumber, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and diced
  • 1/2 cup coarsely chopped skinned and toasted hazelnuts
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest (from about 2 lemons)

Vinaigrette

  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice (from 1 to 2 lemons)
  • 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Directions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high heat. Add the lentils and cook until tender, stirring occasionally, about 18 to 20 minutes. Drain and let cool for 5 minutes. Place lentils and remaining salad ingredients in a large salad bowl.
  2. Place the lemon juice in a small bowl. Slowly add the oil, whisking constantly, until combined. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  3. Pour the vinaigrette over the salad and toss well.

Sesame-Thyme Crusted Goat Cheese and Arugula Salad with Warm Shiitake-Tomato Vinaigrette

Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 log goat cheese, cut into 1/2-inch slices
  • 1/2 cup sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, minced, plus sprigs for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon coarse cracked black pepper
  • 1 pound frisée
  • 2 tablespoons Pommery mustard (whole grain)
  • 2/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 2 cups sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 cup ripe tomatoes (yellow and red), cut into 1 1/2-inch dice
  • 1 crusty loaf of country bread, cut into 1-inch slices
  • 1 head garlic
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil to cook

Directions

  1. On a plate, mix together the sesame seeds, thyme, and coarse black pepper. Dip the cheese slices on one side only.
  2. In a very hot nonstick pan coated lightly with olive oil, pan-sear the crusted side only until golden brown.
  3. Whisk together the mustard, vinegar, and olive oil. Season and check for flavor.
  4. In a hot sauté pan coated lightly with olive oil, sauté the shallots and shiitakes until soft, about 3 minutes. Add the tomatoes and deglaze with the vinaigrette; check for seasoning.
  5. In a large bowl, toss the arugula with half of the vinaigrette and check for flavor. Add more vinaigrette if needed.
  6. Slice the head of garlic in half and lightly rub the bread with the garlic; grill until golden brown.
  7. To plate: Lay down a slice of bread and place a tight mound of the salad on top. Top with warm goat cheese. Garnish with 1/4 cup basil leaves.

Tomato Salad with Sesame Crusted Chicken and Mozzarella

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 12 chicken tenders
  • 2/3 cup black and plain sesame seeds
  • 5 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6 medium tomatoes, halved and cored
  • 8 ounces fresh baby spinach or mixed baby greens
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 ball fresh burrata mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • 1 tablespoon grapeseed oil

Directions

  1. Coat chicken tenders in the sesame seeds. Heat 1 tablespoon grapeseed oil in a large nonstick sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add half the sesame-coated chicken tenders and cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until just cooked through and golden brown. Transfer to paper towels. Repeat with remaining oil and chicken.
  2. Whisk the vinegar and remaining 3 tablespoons olive oil in a medium bowl to blend; season the vinaigrette with salt and pepper.
  3. Cut the tomatoes in half, then add to a large bowl with the baby spinach and red onion. Toss with the vinaigrette and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Divide the salad among 4 serving plates. Top with sesame-crusted chicken and torn pieces of burrata.

Fuji Apple, Walnut and Herb Salad with Roasted Chicken

Serves: 4

Dressing

  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon prepared mustard (such as Dijon)
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Salad

  • 1 cup walnut pieces
  • 1 cup cored and chopped apple
  • 2 tablespoons Italian seasoning spice blend or dried dill
  • 1 cup shredded cooked/roasted chicken (use leftovers)

Directions

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the lemon zest, yogurt, oil, mustard, vinegar, lemon juice, salt, and pepper, and mix well.
  2. In another medium bowl, combine the walnuts, apple chunks, dill, and cooked chicken.
  3. Pour the dressing onto the apple mixture right before serving and stir until blended. Arrange on serving plates and serve chilled.

Open-Faced “Tostada” Salads

Cook: 7–8 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 whole wheat tortillas
  • 1/2 cup frozen corn, thawed
  • 1 can kidney beans, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup cooked brown rice
  • 6 ounces mild cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 head of lettuce, chopped, or 1/2 cup salad greens
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup salsa, if desired
  • 2 tablespoons chopped scallions
  • Lime zest (optional)
  • Handful chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place 2 tortillas in an ovenproof dish.
  2. Add corn, beans, and rice to cover the tortillas and sprinkle with cheese.
  3. Bake until the cheese melts and the beans bubble around the edges, about 7 to 8 minutes.
  4. Remove from oven and top with chopped lettuce, tomato, salsa, scallions, and cilantro. Sprinkle with lime zest if desired.
  5. Cut into 4 portions and serve immediately.

Wilted Butter Lettuce, Fresh Garden Peas and Beurre Blanc

Serves: 4

Beurre Blanc

  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 6 whole black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 stick cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • Kosher salt

Bibb Lettuce Salad

  • 1 large head butter lettuce
  • 1 cup fresh garden peas, blanched in salted water

Directions

  1. Beurre blanc: Add white wine, shallot, bay leaf, peppercorns, and salt to a saucepan. Simmer until syrupy and just coating the bottom of the pan.
  2. Add butter cubes a little at a time, whisking constantly, to thicken the sauce and give it a shine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Salad: Cut off the root of the butter lettuce and separate the leaves. Place leaves in a large mixing bowl with blanched peas, then dress with warm beurre blanc and toss so the leaves wilt a little.
  4. To serve the complete dish, add chicken and potatoes to the lettuce salad and gently toss to combine. Finish with a drizzle of beurre blanc.

Broccoli Slaw with Cranberries

Serves: 4

Slaw

  • 1 bag ready-cut broccoli slaw mix, or 2 heads broccoli (heads removed, stems julienned)
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 red onion, julienned
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries

Dressing

  • 1 cup low-fat mayonnaise
  • 1 cup low-fat sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Combine all dressing ingredients in a mixing bowl and whisk until well combined.
  2. When just ready to eat, dress vegetables with dressing and finish with a drizzle of olive oil.
  3. Scatter dried cranberries over the top to finish.

Chicken Salad with Potatoes and Arugula

Total: 20 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound new potatoes
  • Kosher salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 (2 1/2- to 3-pound) rotisserie chicken
  • 3 bunches arugula, thick stems removed (about 6 cups)
  • 1/4 cup fresh tarragon leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan, shaved (2 ounces)

Directions

  1. Place the potatoes in a large pot, cover with cold water, and bring to a boil. Add 1 1/2 tablespoons salt, reduce heat, and simmer until tender, 12 to 15 minutes. Drain, run under cold water to cool, and slice.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the oil, vinegar, mustard, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper.
  3. Using a fork or your fingers, shred the chicken, discarding the skin and bones.
  4. In individual bowls, combine the arugula, chicken, tarragon, and potatoes. Drizzle with the dressing and sprinkle with the Parmesan.

Red Grapefruit and Fennel Salad

Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 3 ruby-red grapefruit
  • 1/4 teaspoon whole fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small red onion
  • 1 small fennel bulb
  • 8 ounces baby spinach or watercress

Directions

  1. Using a paring knife, remove peel, pith, and outer membranes from 2 grapefruit. Slice into 1/4-inch rounds; set aside. Squeeze juice from the remaining grapefruit and set aside.
  2. Place fennel seeds in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook until golden brown and fragrant, about 3 minutes. Transfer to a spice grinder and grind until very fine.
  3. Place ground seeds in a bowl; add 1/4 cup grapefruit juice, honey, and mustard. Season with salt and pepper. Whisk to combine, slowly adding olive oil. Set dressing aside.
  4. Using a mandoline or sharp knife, slice onion into thin rounds; place in a bowl. Pour remaining grapefruit juice over onions; toss.
  5. Cut fennel in half lengthwise; remove core. Using a mandoline, slice fennel. Drain onions; place in a large bowl. Add fennel, spinach, and grapefruit rounds. Toss with dressing.

Roger Mooking’s Quinoa Salad

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups quinoa, cooked according to package directions
  • 1/2 bunch green onion, whites smashed and finely sliced, green tops chopped
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 English cucumber, halved and diced
  • 1/2 bunch mint, picked and roughly chopped
  • Salt

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, place whites of green onion, lemon juice, zest, ginger, oil, and salt; stir to incorporate.
  2. In a large bowl, place quinoa, cucumber, and chopped green onion tops.

Tomato Soup with Pancetta

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 ounces pancetta, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 (3/4-inch-thick) slices rustic rye bread (each about 5 3/4 by 3 3/4 inches), cubed
  • 6 cups canned low-salt chicken broth
  • 1 (28-ounce) can diced tomatoes with juices
  • 1/4 cup coarsely chopped fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried crushed red pepper flakes
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream

Directions

  1. Heat the oil in a heavy large pot over medium heat. Add the pancetta and sauté until crisp and golden, about 5 minutes. Add the onion and sauté until tender, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the bread and toss to coat with the pan drippings. Sauté until the bread is crisp and golden, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the broth, tomatoes, basil, oregano, and crushed red pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer until the flavors blend, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stir the mascarpone and sour cream in a small bowl to blend. Ladle the soup into bowls. Spoon a dollop of the mascarpone mixture atop each and serve.

Cioppino

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large fennel bulb, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 large shallots, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 large garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 3/4 teaspoon dried crushed red pepper flakes, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 (28-ounce) can diced tomatoes in juice
  • 1 1/2 cups dry white wine
  • 5 cups fish stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pound Manila clams, scrubbed
  • 1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 pound uncooked large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 1/2 pounds assorted firm-fleshed fish fillets (such as halibut or salmon), cut into 2-inch chunks

Directions

  1. Heat the oil in a very large pot over medium heat. Add the fennel, onion, shallots, and salt and sauté until the onion is translucent, about 10 minutes. Add the garlic and 3/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes and sauté 2 minutes.
  2. Stir in the tomato paste. Add tomatoes with their juices, wine, fish stock, and bay leaf. Cover and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer until the flavors blend, about 30 minutes.
  3. Add the clams and mussels to the cooking liquid. Cover and cook until they begin to open, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the shrimp and fish. Simmer gently until the fish and shrimp are just cooked through and the clams are completely open, stirring gently, about 5 minutes longer. Discard any clams and mussels that do not open. Remove bay leaf.
  5. Season to taste with more salt and red pepper flakes. Ladle into bowls and serve.

Carrot Soup with Orange and Tarragon

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 (1-pound) bag classic-cut peeled carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 3 cups low-salt chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon brandy
  • 2 teaspoons chopped fresh tarragon, plus sprigs for garnish

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a heavy large pot over medium heat. Add carrots and onion; sauté until onion is soft, about 8 minutes.
  2. Add broth; cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, uncover, and simmer until carrots are tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Working in batches, purée soup in blender until very smooth. Return soup to pot.
  4. Stir in orange juice, brandy, and chopped tarragon. Simmer 5 minutes for flavors to blend. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with tarragon sprigs and serve.

Simplest Butternut Squash Soup

Cook: 25–35 min
Serves: 6

Soup

  • 1 butternut squash (about 3 pounds), peeled, seeded, and cut into 1- to 2-inch chunks
  • 3 slender or 1 1/2 larger leeks, white parts only, split lengthwise, washed, and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • Fresh grated nutmeg

Optional Garnish

  • 1 tart apple, peeled, cored, and cut into tiny dice
  • About 1/3 cup chopped toasted hazelnuts or walnuts
  • About 1/2 cup crème fraîche or heavy cream

Directions

  1. Toss squash into a large Dutch oven or soup pot. Add the leeks, milk, and water; salt generously and bring to a boil.
  2. Lower heat to a simmer and cook 25 to 35 minutes, or until squash is soft enough to mash when pressed lightly with the back of a spoon.
  3. Using a blender or food processor, purée the soup in batches until very smooth. Thin to desired consistency with milk or water. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Reheat if necessary — this soup is at its best when truly hot.
  4. Ladle into bowls, spoon over the apples and nuts, and garnish with a little crème fraîche or cream.

Note: The dear friend in Paris who gave me this recipe told me it was embarrassingly simple. And it is — but its rich flavors will make any home cook proud.

Lentil Sausage Soup

Cook: 1 hr 30 min
Level: Easy
Yield: 4 quarts (8–10 servings)

Soup

  • 1 pound French green lentils (recommended: du Puy)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus extra for serving
  • 4 cups diced yellow onions (3 large)
  • 4 cups chopped leeks, white and light green parts only (2 leeks)
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (2 large cloves)
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 3 cups medium-diced celery (8 stalks)
  • 3 cups medium-diced carrots (4 to 6 carrots)
  • 3 quarts homemade chicken stock or canned broth
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 pound kielbasa, cut in half lengthwise and sliced 1/3-inch thick
  • 2 tablespoons dry red wine or red wine vinegar
  • Freshly grated Parmesan, for serving

Homemade Chicken Stock (optional)

  • 3 (5-pound) chickens
  • 3 large onions, unpeeled and quartered
  • 6 carrots, unpeeled and halved
  • 4 celery stalks with leaves, cut in thirds
  • 4 parsnips, unpeeled and cut in half (optional)
  • 20 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 15 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 20 sprigs fresh dill
  • 1 head garlic, unpeeled and cut in half crosswise
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons whole black peppercorns

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, cover the lentils with boiling water and allow to sit for 15 minutes. Drain.
  2. In a large stockpot over medium heat, heat the olive oil and sauté the onions, leeks, garlic, salt, pepper, thyme, and cumin for 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are translucent and tender.
  3. Add the celery and carrots and sauté for another 10 minutes.
  4. Add the chicken stock, tomato paste, and drained lentils; cover and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer uncovered for 1 hour, or until the lentils are cooked through and tender. Check the seasonings.
  5. Add the kielbasa and red wine and simmer until the kielbasa is hot.
  6. Serve drizzled with olive oil and sprinkled with grated Parmesan.

Chicken stock: Place all stock ingredients in a 16- to 20-quart stockpot with 7 quarts of water and bring to a boil. Skim the surface as needed. Simmer uncovered for 4 hours. Strain through a colander, discarding solids, and chill. Discard hardened fat and pack in quart containers. Yields 6 quarts.

Minestrone

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup dried cannellini beans
  • 2 cups hot tap water
  • 1 1/2 pounds ripe plum (Roma) tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 large yellow onion, cut into 1/2-inch dice
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch dice
  • 2 celery stalks, cut into 1/2-inch dice
  • 3 or 4 small potatoes (preferably Yellow Finn or Yukon Gold), peeled and cut into 3/4-inch dice
  • 2 or 3 fresh oregano sprigs
  • 6 cups chicken stock
  • 4 or 5 green Swiss chard leaves, washed, stems removed, cut into 1/4-inch strips
  • 1 cup elbow macaroni or other short dried pasta
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 8–10 fresh basil leaves, cut into fine shreds
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano

Directions

  1. Sort through the beans, discarding any discolored ones. Rinse, drain, and place in a saucepan with hot water to cover by 2 inches. Bring to a boil, immediately remove from heat, cover, and let stand 1 hour. Drain, rinse, and drain again.
  2. Return beans to the pan and add 2 cups hot tap water. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until tender, 1 to 1 1/2 hours. Set aside.
  3. Core and peel the tomatoes. Cut in half crosswise and squeeze out the seeds. Chop the tomatoes (you should have 2 1/2 to 3 cups). Set aside.
  4. In a large saucepan over medium-low heat, melt the butter. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, 6 to 7 minutes. Add the carrots and celery and sauté until beginning to soften, 4 to 5 minutes.
  5. Add the potatoes, tomatoes, oregano, and chicken stock; raise the heat and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until potatoes are almost tender, about 20 minutes.
  6. Add the chard and cannellini beans with their liquid; stir well. Cover and simmer until chard is tender, about 15 minutes.
  7. Add the pasta, cover, and simmer until pasta is tender, about 15 minutes longer. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  8. Ladle into warmed bowls. Sprinkle with basil and Parmigiano-Reggiano.
