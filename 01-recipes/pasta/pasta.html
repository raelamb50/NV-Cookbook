Homemade Macaroni and Four Cheeses

Cook: 30 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound penne pasta or other tube macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 ounces chopped pancetta
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • Gray salt
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • 4 ounces grated Asiago cheese
  • 4 ounces Cheddar cheese
  • 3 ounces grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 4 ounces Cambozola cheese, in pieces
  • 1 cup bread crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons toasted pine nuts
  • Fresh nutmeg

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Cook pasta until al dente, about 8 minutes, and set aside after draining well.
  2. Warm a sauté pan. Melt butter and sauté chopped pancetta for extra richness.
  3. Add onions and sweat for 4–5 minutes, seasoning with salt. Sprinkle flour over onions to soak up butter — this will create a stable sauce.
  4. Add whipping cream and warm sauce for 1 minute. Lower the heat and add Asiago, Cheddar, and half of the Parmesan cheese. Stir gently and add Cambozola pieces.
  5. Toss cooked, well-drained macaroni in cheese sauce and place in a buttered casserole dish.
  6. Mix remaining Parmesan with bread crumbs and sprinkle on top. Cover top with freshly ground pepper, toasted pine nuts, and fresh grated nutmeg.
  7. Bake until golden brown and crispy on top, about 30 minutes.

Yogurt Cheese — A Healthy Substitute for Cream Cheese

Ingredients

  • Plain yogurt (make sure it does not contain gelatin)
  • Yogurt cheese funnel or paper-lined coffee filter

Directions

  1. Place funnel or paper-lined coffee filter over a large measuring cup. Spoon in plain yogurt.
  2. Cover and refrigerate. Let drain for about 14 hours to get a thick, creamy consistency.
  3. Use yogurt cheese as a base for dips or in place of sour cream. For a sweet spread, flavor with maple syrup; for a savory version, add salt, pepper, and fresh herbs.

Tip: Rich-tasting yogurt cheese is yogurt from which the whey has been drained, leaving it soft and spreadable. It’s low in fat — fat-free if you start with fat-free yogurt.

Five Easy Ravioli Sauces

Serves: 4 each

Cherry-Tomato Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 teaspoons flour
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • Cheese ravioli

Mushroom Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 cups sliced, stemmed shiitake mushrooms
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 teaspoons flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • Chicken and rosemary ravioli

Oriental Vegetable Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon Oriental sesame oil
  • 1 1/2 cups halved snow peas
  • 1 medium red pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 1 tablespoon each minced ginger and garlic
  • 2 teaspoons flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • Beef and garlic ravioli

Apple-Arugula Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 Granny Smith apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon minced shallot
  • 2 teaspoons flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon each salt and pepper
  • 1 bunch arugula, coarsely chopped
  • Gorgonzola-walnut ravioli

Pea Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons cream
  • 1 cup thawed frozen peas
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh dill
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • Cheese ravioli

Directions

  1. Cherry-Tomato: Heat oil; cook garlic 1 minute. Add tomatoes; cook until soft. Stir in flour, broth, and parsley. Cook over low heat until thick. Toss with cheese ravioli.
  2. Mushroom: In oil, sauté mushrooms, garlic, and scallions until soft. Stir in flour, broth, tomato paste, and pepper; cook over low heat until thick. Toss with chicken and rosemary ravioli.
  3. Oriental Vegetable: In sesame oil, sauté snow peas, red pepper, scallions, ginger, and garlic until soft. Stir in flour and beef broth; cook until thick. Toss with beef and garlic ravioli.
  4. Apple-Arugula: In butter, sauté apples and shallot until soft. Stir in flour, broth, salt, and pepper; cook until thick. Add arugula. Toss with Gorgonzola-walnut ravioli.
  5. Pea: In oil, sauté scallions and garlic until soft. Stir in flour, broth, cream, peas, dill, and pepper. Cook until thick. Toss with cheese ravioli.

Note: Each sauce uses a 9-ounce package of fresh ravioli (found in the supermarket’s refrigerated section). All have around 300 calories and 13 grams of fat per serving.

Ravioli and Asparagus in Goat Cheese Sauce

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter or margarine
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 (3.5-ounce) package Montrachet goat cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon peel
  • 1 bunch asparagus, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 (9-ounce) package chicken and rosemary ravioli
  • Minced sun-dried tomatoes, for garnish

Directions

  1. Make the sauce: In a medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add flour and cook, stirring, 1 minute.
  2. Slowly whisk in the chicken broth and cook until the sauce lightly thickens, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and drop in the goat cheese and lemon peel, stirring until the cheese melts. Set sauce aside and keep warm.
  3. Heat a large pot of water to boiling and salt it lightly. Drop asparagus into boiling water and cook 5 minutes until fork-tender. Remove with slotted spoon and set aside.
  4. Add ravioli to boiling water and cook according to label directions. Drain.
  5. Combine ravioli with asparagus and toss with sauce. Garnish with minced sun-dried tomatoes.

Pasta with Tomatoes and Eggplant

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large clove garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) Italian-style peeled tomatoes, well drained
  • 1 tablespoon dry white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc
  • 1 tablespoon sherry wine vinegar or red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sun-dried tomato paste (available in jars or tubes), optional but highly recommended
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 pound tender young eggplant, preferably Japanese eggplant, stems removed
  • 1 pound penne or mostaccioli pasta
  • 4 ounces feta cheese, drained and crumbled
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 4–6 fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Directions

  1. To make tomato sauce: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for about 1 minute or just until it releases its fragrance; do not overcook or garlic will be bitter.
  2. Add tomatoes, increase heat, and simmer uncovered for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally and breaking up tomatoes as you stir.
  3. Add wine and vinegar and cook for 10 minutes. (This is a concentrated sauce — cook mixture until most juices in skillet have evaporated.) Add sun-dried tomato paste and stir to combine. Season with salt.
  4. Meanwhile, cook eggplant and pasta. Toss pasta with tomato sauce, crumbled feta cheese, black pepper, fresh basil leaves, and olive oil.

Light Cheese Ravioli with Roasted Vegetables

Prep: 25 min
Serves: 4 to 6

Ingredients

  • 3/4 pound baby carrots
  • 1/2 pound fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and thickly sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 (7-ounce) container Contadina refrigerated pesto with basil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 1 (9-ounce) package Contadina refrigerated light cheese ravioli, cooked, drained, and kept warm

Directions

  1. Toss vegetables and olive oil in a jelly-roll pan or large baking sheet with sides; spread in an even layer.
  2. Bake at 425°F for 18 to 20 minutes or until tender and lightly browned, stirring occasionally.
  3. Combine pesto, lemon juice, and pepper; toss with ravioli and roasted vegetables.
  4. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Linguine with Hot Garlic Shrimp and Sugar Snap Peas

Prep: 25 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups small sugar snap peas, ends trimmed (about 1/3 pound), or 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 (9-ounce) package Contadina refrigerated tomato & herb linguine, cooked, drained, and kept warm
  • 1 (10-ounce) container Contadina refrigerated light Alfredo sauce

Directions

  1. Toss shrimp, lemon juice, garlic, and red pepper in a small bowl; cover and let stand 15 minutes.
  2. Sauté peas in 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large heavy skillet over medium-high heat until just tender; transfer to a serving bowl and keep warm.
  3. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in skillet; add shrimp mixture. Sauté 4 minutes or until shrimp turn opaque.
  4. Stir in Alfredo sauce; cook 1 minute or until hot, stirring constantly.
  5. Toss with linguine and peas. Serve immediately.

Mushroom Tortelloni with Chicken and Red Bell Pepper Sauce

Prep: 25 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1/2 ounce dried wild mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup dry sherry or vermouth
  • 1 (10-ounce) container Contadina refrigerated red bell pepper cream sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 sweet red pepper, cut into 2 1/4-inch julienne strips
  • 3/4 pound skinned and boned chicken breast halves, cubed
  • 1 (9-ounce) package Contadina refrigerated mushroom tortelloni, cooked, drained, and kept warm
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted

Directions

  1. Simmer mushrooms and sherry 3 minutes in a small saucepan; remove from heat and let stand, covered, 15 minutes. Drain and chop mushrooms, reserving liquid.
  2. Pour liquid through a strainer lined with a dampened paper towel into a bowl; stir in red bell pepper cream sauce.
  3. Sauté pepper strips and mushrooms in 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet until peppers are just tender; transfer to a serving bowl and keep warm.
  4. Heat remaining olive oil in skillet; add chicken and sauté until golden brown over medium-high heat.
  5. Reduce heat to medium and stir in red bell pepper cream sauce mixture. Cook, stirring constantly, until hot.
  6. Toss with tortelloni and pepper mixture in serving bowl; sprinkle with toasted pine nuts.

Seafood Pasta with Lemon-Saffron Herb Dressing

Serves: 6 to 8

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces campanelle (trumpet-shaped pasta) or macaroni
  • 1/8 teaspoon crushed saffron threads
  • 7 tablespoons fresh lemon juice, divided
  • 6 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced green onions
  • 2 tablespoons drained capers
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh tarragon, plus sprigs for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh dill, plus sprigs for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 pound cooked medium shrimp
  • 1 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1/2 pound Dungeness crabmeat
  • 2 large handfuls of arugula
  • 2 small heads of butter lettuce
  • Lemon wedges

Directions

  1. Cook pasta in large pot of boiling salted water until tender but still firm to bite, stirring occasionally. Drain, reserving 1/2 cup cooking liquid.
  2. Place 3 tablespoons hot cooking liquid in a medium bowl. Sprinkle saffron over and let stand at room temperature 5 minutes.
  3. Transfer pasta to a large bowl. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon lemon juice and 1 tablespoon oil; toss to coat. Let stand at room temperature until cool, stirring occasionally.
  4. Whisk remaining lemon juice, remaining oil, mayonnaise, sour cream, green onions, capers, tarragon, dill, and sugar into the saffron mixture.
  5. Add shrimp, celery, and crabmeat to the pasta. Pour dressing over and toss to combine.
  6. Serve over arugula and butter lettuce leaves, garnished with lemon wedges and herb sprigs.

Pasta with Ricotta, Lemon and Spinach

Prep: 12 min
Cook: 30 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces rigatoni, bowties, or other pasta
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice from 2 lemons (about 1/2 cup)
  • 1/4 cup finely grated Parmesan
  • 1/2 cup chopped basil leaves, or 1 tablespoon dried
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (6-ounce) bag or 6 cups fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 8 ounces (1 cup) ricotta

Directions

  1. Cook pasta in a large pot of boiling, salted water according to package directions. Drain and return to pot.
  2. Combine oil, lemon juice, Parmesan, basil, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Add to hot pasta along with spinach leaves and toss well to coat.
  3. Divide among 4 bowls and top each with a dollop of ricotta. Pass extra Parmesan, if desired.

Tip: If your kids are spinach-shy, substitute 2 cups cut cherry tomatoes or 1 cup cooked baby peas.

Penne with Gorgonzola Cream Sauce

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 pound Gorgonzola Dolce cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 pound penne
  • 1/2 cup toasted chopped walnuts
  • Freshly ground nutmeg, to taste

Directions

  1. In a saucepan over low heat, melt butter. Add cream and Gorgonzola, and cook until the Gorgonzola melts completely, stirring continuously. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large pot of boiling water, cook penne until it is tender to the bite. Drain.
  3. Toss with Gorgonzola cream sauce. Serve garnished with toasted walnuts and nutmeg.

Pasta Ricotta Pie

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 50–60 min
Serves: 8

Ingredients

  • 1/4 pound vermicelli
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup grated fresh Romano cheese, divided
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 (16-ounce) container part-skim ricotta cheese
  • 1 (10-ounce) package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 (26-ounce) jar spaghetti sauce

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Break vermicelli into thirds and cook according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic in oil until tender. Add pasta, 1/2 cup Romano cheese, and 1 egg; mix well. Press into a greased 9-inch springform pan.
  3. Separate yolks and whites of remaining 2 eggs. Combine yolks, ricotta cheese, spinach, salt, and remaining 1/2 cup Romano cheese. Spread over pasta layer.
  4. In a small mixing bowl, beat egg whites until stiff but not dry; fold in 1 1/2 cups spaghetti sauce. Pour over spinach layer.
  5. Bake 50 to 60 minutes, until set. Let stand 10 minutes, then remove sides of pan and cut into wedges.
  6. Heat remaining sauce and serve with pie.

Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Serves: 4 to 6

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1/3 pound Italian pancetta, cut into thin strips
  • 3 slices Canadian bacon, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 pound dried spaghetti
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano, freshly grated, divided
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 tablespoons Italian parsley, finely chopped

Directions

  1. Heat butter and 2 tablespoons olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. When butter melts, add pancetta and Canadian bacon. Cook until pancetta is browned but not crisp.
  2. Add white wine. Cook until wine reduces by half. Set aside.
  3. Cook spaghetti with remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in plenty of salted boiling water until al dente.
  4. Meanwhile, beat egg yolks with 2 tablespoons Parmigiano-Reggiano, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  5. Drain spaghetti when cooked and immediately pour over egg-cheese mixture. Add pancetta–Canadian bacon mixture. Toss, making sure to coat spaghetti evenly.
  6. Add remaining 2 tablespoons Parmigiano-Reggiano and Italian parsley. Toss again and serve.

Butternut Squash Lasagna

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 1 hr 25 min
Level: Medium
Serves: 8 to 10

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 (1 1/2 to 2-pound) butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 3 amaretti cookies, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 cups whole milk
  • Pinch nutmeg
  • 3/4 cup (lightly packed) fresh basil leaves
  • 12 no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 2 1/2 cups shredded whole-milk mozzarella cheese
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add squash, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 5 minutes. Add water, cover, and cook until squash is tender, about 15 minutes. Mash with a fork and stir in crumbled amaretti cookies.
  2. Make béchamel: Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook 1 minute. Gradually whisk in milk and cook until sauce thickens. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F. Spread a thin layer of béchamel in a baking dish. Layer noodles, squash mixture, basil leaves, mozzarella, and béchamel. Repeat layers.
  4. Top final layer with remaining béchamel, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
  5. Cover with foil and bake 30 minutes. Uncover and bake until golden and bubbling, about 15 minutes more. Let rest 15 minutes before serving.
