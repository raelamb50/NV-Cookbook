Tomato and Goat Cheese Tarts

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 25 min
Serves: 4 individual tarts

Ingredients

  • 1 package (17.3 ounces / 2 sheets) puff pastry, defrosted
  • Good olive oil
  • 4 cups thinly sliced yellow onions (2 large onions)
  • 3 large garlic cloves, cut into thin slivers
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons dry white wine
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh thyme leaves
  • 4 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan, plus 2 ounces shaved with a vegetable peeler
  • 4 ounces garlic-and-herb goat cheese (recommended: Montrachet)
  • 1 large tomato, cut into 4 (1/4-inch-thick) slices
  • 3 tablespoons julienned basil leaves

Directions

  1. Unfold a sheet of puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and roll it lightly to an 11 by 11-inch square. Using a 6-inch wide saucer as a guide, cut 2 circles from the sheet, discarding the scraps. Repeat with the second sheet to make 4 circles in all. Place on 2 sheet pans lined with parchment paper and refrigerate until ready to use.
  2. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  3. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat and add the onions and garlic. Sauté for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring frequently, until the onions are limp and there is almost no moisture remaining. Add 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, the wine, and thyme and continue to cook for another 10 minutes, until the onions are lightly browned. Remove from heat.
  4. Using a sharp paring knife, score a 1/4-inch-wide border around each pastry circle. Prick the pastry inside the score lines with the tines of a fork and sprinkle a tablespoon of grated Parmesan on each round, staying inside the scored border.
  5. Place 1/4 of the onion mixture on each circle, staying within the scored edge. Crumble 1 ounce of goat cheese on top of the onions. Place a slice of tomato in the center of each tart. Brush the tomato lightly with olive oil and sprinkle with basil, salt, and pepper. Scatter 4 or 5 shards of Parmesan on each tart.
  6. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the pastry is golden brown. The bottom sheet pan may need an extra few minutes in the oven. Serve hot or warm.

Frittata Primavera

Serves: 2

Ingredients

  • 3 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh asparagus pieces
  • 1/2 cup canned or frozen artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1/2 cup sugar snap peas, strings pulled and cut in 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  • 3/4 cup egg substitute or 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon plain low-fat yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. Heat 1 teaspoon of the oil in a skillet and cook the onion 2 or 3 minutes or until soft. Add the garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  2. Stir in the asparagus, artichoke hearts, peas, basil, and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender but still slightly crisp, 3 to 5 minutes. Set aside.
  3. Preheat the broiler. Beat the egg substitute (or eggs) with the yogurt and another pinch of pepper.
  4. Heat the remaining oil in a heavy-bottomed skillet. Pour in the egg mixture and cook until just set on the bottom but still wet on the top, about 1 minute.
  5. Scatter the vegetables over the top and set under the broiler to finish cooking, about 2 minutes. Dust the top with Parmesan cheese, cut into wedges, and serve.

Note: Provides 1 1/2 vegetable servings per person.

Spinach Pie

Prep: 20 min
Cook: 60 min
Serves: 8

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces frozen cut leaf spinach, thawed
  • 5 eggs
  • 2 pounds fresh ricotta
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill, or 1 teaspoon dried (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 ounces (1/4 cup) cherry or grape tomatoes, halved
  • 4 ounces feta, roughly crumbled

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Squeeze out all liquid from thawed spinach and set aside.
  2. Beat eggs well in a large bowl, then thoroughly blend in spinach, ricotta, and dill (if using). Season with salt and pepper and spread into a lightly greased 3-quart casserole.
  3. Place tomatoes, cut side up, on spinach mixture, then sprinkle crumbled feta over top.
  4. Bake about 1 hour, until pie is set and lightly golden. Serve warm, cold, or at room temperature.

Tip: Thaw the spinach the night before, and then whip up this Greek-inspired family treat — no crust needed! The leftovers are great to take to work.

Spinach and Cheese Quiche

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 30 min
Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 1 packaged rolled pie pastry, such as Pillsbury
  • 1 (10-ounce) box frozen leaf spinach
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups half-and-half
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 4 ounces Swiss cheese, shredded (1 cup)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Unroll pastry according to package directions. Invert a 9-inch cake pan onto a baking sheet; drape pastry over pan, centering dough as well as possible. Gently pierce flat portion of surface with fork to prevent bubbles.
  2. Bake for about 10 minutes, then reduce heat to 350°F and bake until golden and crisp, about 15 minutes more. Transfer baking sheet to a rack and let cool.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F. Wrap a sheet of foil around outside of a cake pan to form a foil shell. Place foil shell right-side up on a baking sheet. Spray with cooking spray. Place pastry shell inside foil to prevent leaking.
  4. Place spinach on a plate and microwave on high for 3 minutes, until thawed but not hot. Squeeze as much liquid as possible out of spinach, then coarsely chop. Transfer to a bowl.
  5. Add eggs, half-and-half, salt, and pepper, and stir with a fork until well blended.
  6. Distribute cheese in bottom of pastry shell. Slowly pour egg and spinach mixture into shell.
  7. Bake until center is set, about 30 minutes. Let cool for 15 minutes before cutting into wedges.

Bacon Home Fries

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 15 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 pound frozen oven fries (preferably with olive oil), cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup packaged crumbled bacon (such as Hormel)
  • Freshly ground pepper

Directions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large, heavy skillet over medium heat. Add onion; cook, stirring, until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons oil and fries; cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and crisp, about 8 minutes.
  3. Add crumbled bacon; cook for 1 minute more. Season to taste with pepper.

Potatoes Savoyarde

Cook: 60–70 min
Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted, plus 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 2 1/2 pounds varietal potatoes, unpeeled and cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • 1 1/2 cups stock

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Brush a 2-quart baking dish with melted butter.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together garlic, parsley, cheese, and pepper.
  3. Layer one-third of the potatoes in the prepared dish and sprinkle one-third of the garlic-cheese mixture over the top. Cover with 1 tablespoon of the butter pieces. Repeat the layers once and finish with the remaining potatoes.
  4. Pour the stock and then the remaining garlic-cheese mixture over the layers. Dot with the remaining butter pieces.
  5. Cover the dish with a piece of aluminum foil buttered on one side. Bake for 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil and continue baking until the potatoes are tender and the top is brown and crusty, 30–40 minutes longer. Serve immediately.

Onion and Bacon Tart

Cook: 25 min
Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 8 bacon slices, chopped
  • 5 cups sliced onions (about 3 large)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • 1 (13.8-ounce) tube refrigerated pizza dough
  • 1/4 teaspoon caraway seeds

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Sauté bacon in a heavy large skillet over medium-high heat until slightly crisp. Drain all but 1 tablespoon bacon drippings from skillet.
  2. Add onions to bacon and sauté over medium heat until onions are very tender but not brown, about 20 minutes. Cool.
  3. Whisk egg, sour cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg in a large bowl to blend. Stir in cooled onion mixture.
  4. Roll pizza dough out on a lightly floured surface to a 13x10-inch rectangle. Transfer to baking sheet.
  5. Spread onion mixture over dough, leaving a 1/2-inch plain border around edges. Sprinkle with caraway seeds.
  6. Bake until onion custard is set and crust is golden brown around edges and brown on bottom, about 25 minutes.

Maple-Glazed Autumn Carrots with Garlic Cloves

Serves: 6 to 8

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds orange and yellow carrots, peeled, halved lengthwise, and cut crosswise into 4-inch-long pieces
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 20 garlic cloves, halved lengthwise
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

Directions

  1. Position 1 rack in the upper third of an oven and another rack in the lower third. Preheat to 400°F.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the carrots, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper and stir to coat evenly. Divide the carrot mixture between 2 baking sheets.
  3. Roast, without stirring, until the carrots and garlic are tender and golden brown underneath, 28 to 30 minutes; halfway through the roasting time, switch the positions of the baking sheets on the oven racks.
  4. Transfer the carrot mixture to a large bowl, drizzle with the maple syrup, and stir to coat evenly. Transfer to a serving bowl and sprinkle with the parsley.

Green Beans with Toasted Almond Gremolata

Yield: 8–10 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 2 tablespoons sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon rind
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced

Directions

  1. Steam beans, covered, 7 minutes or until crisp-tender. Drain and return to pan.
  2. Add parsley, almonds, lemon rind, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic; toss gently to combine.
  3. Serve immediately.

Lemon Roasted Fingerling Potatoes

Cook: 25–30 min
Serves: 4–6

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fingerling potatoes
  • 1/2 Meyer lemon, cut into quarter wedges
  • 1 sprig rosemary, leaves only
  • 2 sprigs thyme, leaves only
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Wash and split fingerling potatoes lengthwise.
  2. Toss in olive oil together with rosemary, thyme, and lemon wedges in a flat roasting tray. Season with salt and freshly ground pepper.
  3. Roast under the rotisserie chicken for 25 to 30 minutes until golden brown. Stir occasionally to coat in chicken drippings and cook evenly.
  4. Season one more time with salt and pepper before serving.

Pan-Grilled Flatbread with Mushrooms and Autumn Vegetables

Serves: 6

Flatbread Filling

  • 6 medium golden beets
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon Madras curry powder
  • 1 leek, white part only, finely chopped
  • 2 large red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup maitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 8 Brussels sprouts, thinly sliced on a mandoline
  • 1/4 pound ricotta salata, thinly sliced
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup plain yogurt

Grilled Flatbread

  • 1 teaspoon dry active yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 1 cup bread flour, plus more for work surface
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus more for bowl and griddle

Grilled Flatbread

  1. In a large bowl, mix together yeast and 3/4 cup water; let stand 5 minutes. Add remaining 1/2 cup water, flour, sugar, salt, and olive oil; mix until a dough is formed.
  2. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead until firm, about 5 minutes. Lightly oil a large bowl and add dough. Cover with plastic wrap and keep in a warm place; let rise until doubled in size, about 2 hours.
  3. Divide dough into 8 to 10 (2-ounce) pieces. Roll each piece out to a 1/8-inch-thick circle; cover dough with a clean towel.
  4. Heat griddle over high heat and lightly coat with olive oil. Working with one piece at a time, add dough to griddle. Cook until bubbling and brown, 40 seconds to 1 minute, then turn and continue cooking 20 seconds more. Remove from heat; wipe griddle clean. Repeat with remaining dough.

Filling & Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place beets in a medium roasting pan and add 1/4 cup water; season with 1 tablespoon salt. Cover with parchment-lined aluminum foil and roast until tender and easily pierced with a paring knife, about 25 minutes. Peel skin using a paper towel. Thinly slice beets and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat butter and curry powder over medium heat. Add leeks and cook, stirring, until tender, 6 to 7 minutes. Add peppers and cook until tender, about 10 minutes. Add mushrooms and cook until steaming and heated through, 5 to 6 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. In a medium bowl, add Brussels sprouts, ricotta salata, lemon juice, and olive oil; season with salt and pepper and toss. In a small bowl, whisk yogurt with a splash of water until smooth; season with salt and pepper.
  4. Lay one piece of flatbread on work surface. Place 3 slices of beets over three-quarters of the flatbread; top with 1/4 cup sautéed vegetables, 2 tablespoons Brussels sprouts, and 1 tablespoon yogurt. Starting with the covered portion, roll flatbread toward the uncovered portion and place seam-side up; insert a skewer to secure. Repeat with remaining flatbread.
  5. Remove skewers and slice each in half before serving.

Cheese Crackers

Yield: About 48 crackers

Ingredients

  • 3 oz extra-sharp cheddar cheese, coarsely grated
  • 1 oz Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
  • 1/2 cup white whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into tablespoons
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne
  • 1/4 teaspoon dry mustard
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons sour cream

Directions

  1. Pulse both cheeses, both flours, butter, salt, cayenne, and dry mustard in a food processor until mixture resembles coarse meal. Add sour cream and pulse until dough just comes together.
  2. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide in half. Roll each half into a log about 1 1/4 inches in diameter. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm, at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  4. Slice logs into 1/4-inch-thick rounds and arrange on prepared baking sheets about 1 inch apart. Bake until golden, 15 to 17 minutes. Transfer to wire racks to cool.
